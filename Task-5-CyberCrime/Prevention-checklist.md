##Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

##1. Password Security

You should use passwords that're really strong and unique. This means you need to use a mix of letters and numbers and symbols.

You should also enable two-factor authentication whenever you can.

##2. Safe Internet Usage

When you are on the internet you should only visit websites that you trust and that are secure.

You can tell if a website is secure because it will say HTTPS.

You should never click on links or pop-ups that you do not know.

##3. Email & Message Safety

If you get an email that looks suspicious do not open it.

Also do not open any attachments that you're not sure about.

Before you respond to an email or click on a link you should verify the identity of the person who sent it.

This means you should check to make sure the email is really from who it says it is.

##4. Device Protection

You should install antivirus software on your device. Keep it updated.

You should also regularly update your operating system and your apps.

This will help keep your device safe from cybercrime.

##5. Personal Information Safety

You should never share your one time password or your passwords or your bank details with anyone.

You should also be careful when you are sharing information on social media.

Cybercrime prevention is about being careful with your cybercrime information and your cybercrime safety.

So you should always be careful, with your information and your passwords and your devices to prevent cybercrime.
